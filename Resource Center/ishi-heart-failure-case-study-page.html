Executing Heart Failure Care Between Visits

A virtual care model enabling continuous, guideline-directed therapy between visits.

The challenge in heart failure care is no longer knowing what to prescribe. It is ensuring those therapies are executed consistently between visits.

Where Heart Failure Care Breaks Down

The gap is not knowledge. It is execution.

Clinical guidelines are well established. Outcomes improve with timely titration and monitoring. But in practice, care is inconsistent.

Many patients remain on suboptimal therapy. Adjustments that should happen weekly occur months apart, or not at all.

Between visits, care breaks down.

A Model Built for Continuous Care

ISHI was designed to execute physician-directed care plans continuously, not just during office visits.

Through a combination of continuous monitoring, AI-enabled prioritization, and a dedicated care team, ISHI enables structured, ongoing intervention between visits.

Monitor

Continuous Data Collection

Biometric data such as blood pressure, heart rate, weight, and device signals are monitored between visits.

Triage

Risk Prioritization

AI-enabled prioritization helps identify patients who require clinical attention.

Act

Protocol-Driven Intervention

A clinical care team executes structured interventions, including medication titration and follow-up.

Care plans are not replaced. They are carried out consistently.

ISHI extends cardiology workflows beyond the clinic while preserving physician oversight and alignment with the primary cardiologist.

Study Overview

This evaluation reflects real-world complexity, not controlled academic conditions.

Population

747 patients across six community cardiology practices in California and Arizona.

Follow-Up

Median enrollment duration of 124 days.

Patient Profile

Mean age of 75.8 years, 37% female, and mixed LVEF population.

Clinical Outcomes

Protocol-driven care enabled consistent improvement across all guideline-directed medical therapy classes.

Improved GDMT Utilization

33.9% → 46.5%ACEi/ARB/ARNI
14.9% → 28.4%SGLT2 inhibitors
19.5% → 22%MRA

Improved Stability

Acute weight gain events declined over time, suggesting earlier intervention and improved fluid management.

What This Changes

ISHI creates a new model for heart failure care by extending specialty care beyond the clinic, improving adherence to guideline-directed therapy, and reducing reliance on in-person follow-up.

For Patients

More stable condition through earlier intervention, continuous monitoring between visits, and improved adherence to prescribed therapy.

For Providers

No added workload, structured workflows, and confidence that care plans are being executed.

For Health Systems

More consistent guideline adherence, scalable care delivery, and a foundation for value-based care.

What This Study Demonstrates

This study demonstrates that protocol-driven virtual care can be executed reliably, enabling high-frequency intervention without workflow disruption.

Three Capabilities Validated

Clinical Effectiveness

Protocol-driven virtual care can improve GDMT utilization and patient stability.

Operational Viability

A centralized care team can manage high volumes of patient data and alerts efficiently.


Provider-Aligned Delivery Model

Care can be extended between visits without disrupting or burdening cardiologists.

What This Study Has Not Yet Evaluated

  • Scalability across larger populations or health systems
  • Unit economics, including cost per patient, margin, and reimbursement model
  • Performance consistency across diverse practice settings

These represent the next phase of validation as the model expands.

Future Direction

ISHI is continuing to evaluate performance across broader patient populations, care team efficiency, scalability, economic impact, and reimbursement models.

Expansion into additional cardiology practices and health system settings is underway to evaluate performance at larger population sizes.

A Shift from Episodic to Continuous Care

Heart failure care does not fail because we lack the right treatments. It fails because those treatments are not consistently executed.

ISHI enables a shift from episodic care to consistent execution of therapy, extending cardiology workflows beyond the clinic and ensuring that evidence-based treatment plans are carried out in real-world settings.
